Strengths

Below are some strengths I have acquired throughout my life and continue to develop as time passes.


Gratitude

I have learned to be grateful for the pleasures that I have been blessed with.
I also make it a point to show appreciation when others are kind or thoughtful to me.
Over the years, I have continued to develop this strength through
continuous reflection on all the good things in my life.
I realised that it is much easier to be happy when I focus on what I have to be grateful for
instead of constantly thinking about everything that's going wrong.

Organization

I have also developed the skill of being organized,
through the use of folders, lists and calendars, alarms, and colour-coding.
I don't have the best memory, so being organized really helps me to handle my
responsibilities. Of course, it is tempting to get lazy and slack off, but I always
remind myself of how much easier life is when I put the effort into staying organized.
This is especially useful whenever I need to find something or plan an event.

Empathy

Lastly is the strength of being empathetic. Of the three, this quality is currently the
least developed, as it is sometimes hard to empathise with how another person is feeling,
especially if they're going through something I have never experienced for myself.
However, I have been working on improving my empathetic abilities by
trying to imagine myself in people's situations and to see things from their point of view.
I can definitely see how my increasing empathy has positively affected my relationships with others.



Reflection on
Twelve Pillars

As part of my personal development, I read Chapters 4 to 7 of the book Twelve Pillars, written by motivational speakers Jim Rohn and Chris Widener. In the book, a middle-aged salesman named Michael receives counsel from an older man named Charlie, who does estate maintenance for Mr Davis, a rich businessman. Michael is unsatisfied with his life and Charlie has been sharing with him Mr. Davis' secrets to success (or pillars). The four pillars covered in Chapters 4 to 7 are setting goals, time management, surrounding yourself with the best people, and being a lifelong learner. Throughout the four chapters, the reader observes that Michael has already started to make progress in working on the previous pillars, and Charlie advises him that in order to maintain and build on this progress, he has to remember to continue putting in effort.

I think I would do well to apply Charlie's advice to my life, especially the part about setting goals. Charlie told Michael that if he aims for nothing, he will achieve nothing. I do have goals for my life, but non-specific ones, and I think it would benefit me to really think about what I want and write them down, like Charlie advised Michael to do. This way, I will have a clear outline of what I want to achieve and this may help keep me focused and determined whenever I am lacking motivation.
